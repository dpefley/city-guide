San Francisco City Guide

This is a city guide for the great city of San Francisco, California!

The San Francisco Skyline, Photo: baycityguide.com

My Four Main Highlights:

  1. Marin Headlands
  2. Oracle Park
  3. Fog Harbor Fish House
  4. Ghiradelli Square

Lombard Street, Photo: accorhotels.com

1. Marin Headlands

Photo: flickr.com/rkroenert

What:

The Marin Headlands are my favorite place to take someone who is visiting the city for the first time. To get there from the city itself, you have to cross the Golden Gate Bridge. Once you've crossed the bridge, follow signs immediately on the other side for the Marin Headlands, and after a bit of a winding vertical road, you'll find yourself overlooking the Golden Gate Bridge and San Francisco, truly a wonderful sight to behold.

When to go:

Sunny days where there is plenty of light. San Francisco has a habit of being very overcast. Make sure to find a beautiful day while you're there!

Cost:

Just the cost of a car and some gas!

Where:
Sausalito, CA 94965, Follow the signs for Marin Headlands.

2. Oracle Park

Photo: sports.yahoo.com

What:

Oracle Park, formerly named AT&T Park, is the home of the San Francisco Giants in Major League Baseball. Winners of the World Series in 2010, 2012, and 2014, they are one of the most storied franchises in American Sports. Snacks are expensive but great, with vegan and carnivore options abound.

When to go:

Baseball Season: April through September; Evening games during the week, some day games during the weekend.

Cost:

Tickets can be had for as little as $8 out in the bleachers, all the way up to $12,000 for a private suite. Food can be expensive with a reasonable meal costing over $20.

Where:
24 Willie Mays Plaza, San Francisco, CA 94107

3. Fog Harbor Fish House

Photo: fogharbor.com

What:

Fog Harbor Fish House is one of my favorite restaurants in San Francisco. Fine dining meets reasonable prices meets amazing views meets Pier 39. Nestled in the upper level of the popular tourist attraction Pier 39, Fog Harbor Fish House hosts large groups and intimate dinners. Offering a full array of seafood dishes coupled with a full bar, it is hard to go wrong at this San Francisco standard.

When to go:
Day Hours
Mon-Thurs 11:00AM-9:00PM
Friday 11:00AM-10:00PM
Saturday 10:30AM-10:00PM
Sunday 10:30AM-9:00PM

Cost:

Starters come in around $15 while entrees range anywhere from $25 to $70.

Where:
Pier 39, San Francisco, CA 94133

4. Ghiradelli Square

Photo: facebook.com/ghiradelli

What:

Ghiradelli Square is a classic tourist attraction. I recommend you walk from Pier 39 all along Fisherman's Wharf to Ghiradelli Square. The Square has parking available, as well as great shopping, and of course, Ghiradelli chocolates! A few places at The Square are:

  • Ghiradelli Chocolate Factory
  • McCormick's & Kuletos
  • The Pub (BBQ)
  • Helpers Bazaar
  • Jackson & Polk

When to go:

11:00AM-9:00PM Every Day

Cost:

Parking is $5 for the first hour, and an additional $8 for each hour more. Validation available.

Where:
900 North Point Street, San Francisco, CA
